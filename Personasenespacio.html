People in space right now.

Sergey Prokopyev

48 years old. On 6 June 2018 he launched on his first flight into space aboard Soyuz MS-09 and spent 197 days in space as a flight engineer on Expedition 56/57.

Dmitri Petelin

40 years old. He flew to the ISS in September 2022 with Soyuz MS-22 and is expected to return to Earth after a year with Soyuz MS-23

Frank Rubio

47 years old. Is a US Army lieutenant colonel and helicopter pilot, flight surgeon, and NASA astronaut. Rubio launched aboard Soyuz MS-22 on September 21, 2022. Broke Mark T. Vande Hei's record for the longest spaceflight by an American astronaut.

Jing Haipeng

56 years old. Is a colonel pilot in the air force of the Chinese People's Liberation Army (PLA), is China’s first three-time astronaut in the country’s manned spaceflight mission.

Gui Haichao

36 years old. Is a Chinese researcher and professor selected as part of the Shenzhou program, is China's first civilian astronaut who is not enlisted in the People's Liberation Army Astronaut Corps.

Zhu Yangzhu

37 years old. Is a Chinese engineer and astronaut selected as part of the Shenzhou program. In June 2022 he was selected as the crew for the manned mission of Shenzhou 16, which launched on 30 May 2023.

Andreas Mogensen

46 years old. Is best known for being the first Dane to fly in space as part of the European Space Agency's iriss program.

Jasmin Moghbeli

40 years old. Is an American U.S. Marine Corps test pilot and NASA astronaut.

Satoshi Furukawa

59 years old. Is a Japanese surgeon and JAXA astronaut.

Konstantin Borisov

39 years old. Is a Russian cosmonaut.
